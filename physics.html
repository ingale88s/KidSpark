🍎 Isaac Newton's Discovery

Legend says Isaac Newton discovered gravity when an apple fell on his head in 1666! He figured out that EVERYTHING with mass pulls on everything else — that's gravity! On the Moon, you'd weigh 6 times LESS because the Moon has less gravity.

🌍 Earth is a Magnet!

The Earth itself is a giant magnet! That's why compasses work — the compass needle is a tiny magnet that aligns with Earth's magnetic field to always point North. Migrating birds and sea turtles can sense Earth's magnetic field to navigate!

⚖️ How Levers Work

A lever has a pivot point called a fulcrum. Weight on one side pushes down, weight on the other side pushes down the other way. When both sides are equal, it balances! Levers are used in seesaws, scissors, wheelbarrows, and even your arm!

4 Friction Race! 🚗

🚗

🛑 Friction is Your Friend!

Without friction you couldn't walk — your shoes would just slide! Car brakes, bicycle brakes, and even pencils writing on paper all need friction. Smooth surfaces like ice have very little friction, while rough surfaces like carpet have lots. That's why roads are made bumpy!

🎢 Roller Coasters Use This!

Real roller coasters start at a tall hill to load up on potential energy. As the coaster drops, all that stored energy turns into speed! That's why the biggest drop makes you go fastest. Elastic energy is similar — stretch a rubber band to store energy, then let go and release it all at once!

🌈 Refraction Creates Rainbows!

When sunlight passes through a raindrop it bends and splits into all the colors — red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and violet! Each color bends a slightly different amount, spreading them into a rainbow arc. Refraction is also how your eyes' lenses focus light so you can see clearly!

🗣️ Your Voice is Vibrations!

Put your fingers gently on your throat and hum — can you feel the buzzing? Those are your vocal cords vibrating! Sound travels through solids, liquids, and gases. In a deep canyon, sound waves bounce off the far wall and come back as an echo — the wider the canyon, the longer the delay! In space there's no air, so it's completely silent.

🔩 Which Metals Are Magnetic?

Only iron, nickel, and cobalt are naturally magnetic. Steel (which is mostly iron) is also magnetic — that's why fridge magnets stick! Gold, silver, aluminum, and copper are NOT magnetic. Next time you're in the kitchen, test your spoons and pots with a fridge magnet to see what's steel and what isn't!
